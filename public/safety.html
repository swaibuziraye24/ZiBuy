🛡️ Safety Tips

Stay safe when buying and selling on ZiBuy Uganda

🤝

Meet in a Safe, Public Place

Always arrange to meet buyers or sellers in busy public locations such as shopping malls, banks, or police stations. Never meet at private residences, especially for first-time transactions. Bring a friend or family member if possible.

🔍

Inspect Before You Pay

Always thoroughly inspect any item before handing over money. Test electronics to ensure they work. Check documents for vehicles. Verify authenticity of branded goods. Never pay for something you haven't seen in person.

💰

Never Pay in Advance

Do not send money to a seller before receiving the item. Legitimate sellers on ZiBuy do not ask for advance payments, deposits, or transfer fees. If someone asks you to pay before meeting, this is a strong warning sign of fraud.

✅

Look for Verified Sellers

ZiBuy verified sellers have submitted identity documents and been approved by our team. Look for the ✅ Verified badge on seller profiles and ads. Verified sellers provide an extra layer of trust and accountability.

📱

Communicate Through ZiBuy

Use ZiBuy's built-in messaging system to communicate with buyers and sellers. This keeps a record of your conversations. Be cautious of sellers who immediately try to move conversations to external platforms or ask for personal details.

📄

Get a Receipt for Vehicles

When buying vehicles, always verify ownership documents (logbook, transfer forms). Ensure the seller's name matches the logbook. Consider using a licensed broker or meeting at the Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) office for vehicle transfers.

🏦

Use Safe Payment Methods

For larger transactions, prefer cash on delivery or bank transfers rather than sending mobile money in advance. Always get confirmation of payment before handing over goods as a seller.

🚨 Common Scams to Watch Out For

  • Sellers asking for M-Pesa/MTN Money transfer before meeting
  • Prices that seem impossibly low for the item
  • Sellers claiming to be overseas and needing shipping fees
  • Requests to move to WhatsApp immediately before showing the item
  • Fake receipts or tampered vehicle documents
  • Buyers sending "proof of payment" screenshots that are fake
  • Requests for your personal ID or bank account details
